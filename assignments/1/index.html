Assignment 1

Upload your answer as “Assignment 1” on Gradescope. An email with information about how to upload will follow.

For this exercise, you will present several arguments, each in the form of a list of two or more sentences — with at least three usually making things easier! — the last of which is the conclusion. The phrases in each sentence should fit together to make the logical relationships among them clear. (See the assigned reading by Salmon for examples of arguments.) Produce your own original examples of arguments, one each with for each of the following characteristics:

  • a deductively valid argument with true premises;
  • a deductively valid argument for the same conclusion as before, but with false premises;
  • an inductively strong argument;
  • a version of that same argument which is even stronger;
  • an argument which sounds a little plausible and might actually persuade someone not being careful about their reasoning, but which is neither deductively valid nor inductively strong.
