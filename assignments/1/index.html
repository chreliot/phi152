Assignment 1

Upload your page to “Assignment 1” on Blackboard, under the Assignments area (link in the left sidebar) by, at the latest, (anytime on) TBD.

Tasks

  1. For this exercise, arguments take the form of two or more sentences — with at least three usually making things easier! — the last of which is the conclusion. The phrases in each sentence should fit together. See forallx, Chapter 2 for examples of arguments. Produce your own original examples of:

    • a deductively valid, sound argument
    • a deductively valid argument which is not sound
    • an inductively strong argument
    • a version of that same argument which is even stronger
    • an argument which sounds a little plausible and might actually persuade someone not being careful about their reasoning, but which is neither deductively valid nor inductively strong

  2. Use your imagination and powers of observation a bit: What are some different ways inductive arguments can have more inductive strength than others? Discuss three ways; write at least 100 words.
