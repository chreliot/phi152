Writing assignment grades

Writing assignments will be scored with a maximum of 40 points. To convert your grade from this raw score to a 4-point scale, simply move the decimal point one place to the left (or, equivalently divide by 10). So, for example, a grade of 27/40 becomes 2.7 out of 4.0, which (following the table above) is equivalent to a B‚Äì letter-scale grade. In this case, the value used to figure the overall course grade (as below) will be the 4-point-scale grade, 2.7. (Why not just post the 4-point grade and not mess with the score out of 40? Test grades

Each answer on tests is scored on an 8-point scale which converts to 4-point scale. The overall test grade is the answers’ scores added together. For example, if there were six test questions, then the highest possible grade would be 48 (= 6 questions x 8 possible points on each question).

The reason I use 8 points per question is that the Blackboard grade book does not allow half grades (like 3.5/4.0), and I think they’re useful. So, when I read answers, I grade on a 4-point scale and then multiply by two. A 3.5/4.0 answer is given 7 points (= 3.5 x 2).

The way to calculate an overall test grade to a 4-point scale is therefore to divide the overall score by the number of questions times 2. For example, if you got a 30/48 on a 6-question test, your 4-point grade would be 30 points divided by (6 questions x 2), which is 2.5. On the 4-point scale, 2.5 is equivalent to a C+.

On this system, your overall grade on the test is just the average of the scores you earned on each question (with the twist that I have to multiply by 2 and then divide by 2).

Overall course grades

Overall course grades will be calculated using the following percentages from the syllabus:

Component Fraction of total
Participation 15%
Assignments 30%
First Test 25%
Second Test 30%

Final grades will be calculated on a 4-point scale and then converted to letter-scale for posting to Hofstra’s final grade system online.
