Topic 2: Early Methods

Learning questions

  1. In New Organon, Bacon develops a new scientific method. We are going to figure out how it works. In the section I assigned you, he describes how to use it to answer the question What is heat? So: How does Bacon investigate what heat is?
  2. What roles do Bacon’s tables of presence, absence, and comparison each play in revealing what can be known about the true nature of something?
  3. Why does Newton object to using hypotheses? What would be the alternative?
  4. What difficulty for inductive reasoning does Hume identify?
  5. Which of the authors above does Whewell reply to?
  6. What is Whewell’s contribution to characterizing the scientific method?
  7. How would you describe Semmelweis’s method, as depicted by Hempel?
  8. Why are confirmation and verification‚Äîfinding evidence in favor of a theory not particularly valuable, according to Popper?
  9. Popper doesn’t use the phrase ‚Äúscientific method‚Äù; even so, what method of reasoning does he prescribe for science?
  10. Between Newton, Whewell, Hempel, and Popper, what considerations are raised for and against hypotheses?

Consider

  1. Is Bacon’s method a good way of learning what heat is?
  2. Is Bacon’s method “the scientific method”? Why or why not?
  3. What can be said for and against hypothesizing being part of scientific method?
  4. Does Hume’s problem make science impossible?
