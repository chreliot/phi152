Topic 6: Causation

We’ve discussed what correlations are, and how they are related to sampling from a population via basic probability ideas. But we noted that correlation isn’t causation, and that researchers are quite often interested in causation. Here we take up how it might be possible to investigate causal relationships, and some of the challenges and limitations inherent to doing that.

Reading

  1. “Understanding and Misunderstanding Randomized Controlled Trials” by Angus Deaton and Nancy Cartwright. Social Science & Medicine 210:2–21 (August 2018). Note: This article has technical parts. I don’t expect you to follow them. Rather, work on reading the beginning and the end, and then parts of the middle that make the argument.
