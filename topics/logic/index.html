Topic 1: Logic

What is logic? What is deductive logic? Could deductive logic be the logic of science? Is non-deductive logic possible?

Reading

(This is what you should read for this section. The due dates appear on the schedule.)

  1. Ian Hacking, An Introduction to Probability and Inductive Logic (purchased book) Chapter 1 “Logic”, whole chapter, pages 1–7.
  2. Robin Smith, “Aristotle’s Logic” in the Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy. Read the 3 paragraph preface then sections 1, 2, 3, and 6.

See also

(Note that items in this section are posted for your possible interest. They are not required. But they might be interesting!)

  1. “Classical Indian Logic” in the Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy
  2. “Logic and Language in Early Chinese Philosophy” in the SEP
  3. “Arabic and Islamic Philosophy of Language and Logic” in the SEP
  4. “Ancient Logic” in the SEP
  5. “Classical Logic” (This is the kind of logic you’d learn in a symbolic logic course like PHI 154, MATH 45, or CSC 14 at Hofstra.)
  6. Logicomix is a graphic novel about the late 19th century search for foundations of logic and math, and some of the personal and social upheaval involved in that. Book jacket:

In his agonized search for absolute truth, [Betrand] Russell crosses paths with legendary thinkers like Gottlob Frege, David Hilbert, and Kurt Gödel, and … Ludwig Wittgenstein. This story is at the same time a historical novel and an accessible explication of some of the biggest ideas of mathematics and modern philosophy.
