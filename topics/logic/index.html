Topic 1: Logic

Could deductive logic be the logic of science? What is logic? And what is deductive logic? Is non-deductive logic possible?

Reading

(This is what you should read for this section. The due dates appear on the front page schedule.)

  1. forallx Calgary: An Introduction to Formal Logic, Fall 2020 edition, by Magnus et al. Get PDF from here, available in several versions; or, here is a direct link to the the screen-friendly PDF version. Read just Chapters 1–2, about 13 pages of text total.
  2. Ian Hacking, An Introduction to Probability and Inductive Logic (purchased book) Chapter 1 “Logic”, whole chapter, pages 1–7.

Handout

  1. Ways of thinking about validity

Learning questions

(These questions reflect some of the main things—not necessarily all the things—I suggest you learn for this section.)

  1. What does deductive logic describe?
  2. What makes an argument deductively valid? … invalid? … sound? … unsound?
  3. What’s an example of a completely deductive scientific method?
  4. What is the difference between deductive and inductive reasoning?
  5. Why would it be a good thing if we could reason scientifically using only deductive logic (and not inductive reasoning)?

Consider

(These more open-ended questions reflect some things I hope this material will have you thinking about for yourself.)

  1. Could a computer reason scientifically? Could one do science?
  2. What’s the relationship between deductive logic and good reasoning more generally?
