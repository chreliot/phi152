Topic 3: Probability

Here we set up some basic ideas about probability which will be key for understanding the approaches to scientific justification which have dominated the last several decades.

Reading

  1. Hacking,
    • Chapter 4 “Elementary Probability Ideas”
    • Chapter 5 “Conditional Probability”
    • Chapter 11 “What Do You Mean?”

See also

  1. Hacking Chapter 3 “The Gambler’s Fallacy” sets up some basic ideas about chance devices, and Chapter 6 “The Basic Rules of Probability” summarizes the rules that appear in the above assigned chapters.
  2. Alan Hájek’s “[Interpretations of Probability][interp]” in the Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy is a much deeper dive than we’ll do in our course into all of this topic, but especially what we land on as we get to Chapter 11 in Hacking.
  3. Here’s an [American Roulette simulator][rousim] implemented in R Shiny
