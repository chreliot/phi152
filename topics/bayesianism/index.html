Topic 4: Bayesianism

Bayesianism, as an approach to inductive reasoning, focuses on updating our beliefs in light of new evidence. It is built around a simple formula that expreses a relationship between probabilities.

Reading

  1. Hacking, Chapter 7 “Bayes' Rule” (pages 69–77)
  2. Hacking, Chapter 13 “Personal Probabilities” (pages 151–160)
  3. Hacking, Chapter 21 “Learning from Experience as an Evasion of the Problem of Induction” (4 pages) — and if you’re not feeling sufficiently familiar with “the problem of induction,” look at the first pages of Chapter 20

See also

  • John Horgan, “Bayes’s Theorem: What’s the Big Deal?,” Scientific American, 2016
  • Elliott Sober, “Introduction to Bayesian Epistemology”
  • Hacking, Chapter 14 “Coherence” (pages 163–169)
